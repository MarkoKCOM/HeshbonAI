# Writing Conventions - HeshbonAI Emails

Rules set by Sione. Apply to ALL email copy, templates, and outreach.

---

## Punctuation

- **SHORT dash only** - use `-` not `—` (em dash is banned)
- No fancy punctuation - keep it plain and readable

## Email Copy Style

- **Lead with pain points** - hit the real problems CPAs face first
- **Feature-forward** - name what the product does, don't be vague
- **Cut the fluff** - every sentence must earn its place
- Short paragraphs - max 2-3 lines per block
- One clear CTA per email - no options, no "or"

## Feature Language Rules

- NEVER say "15-minute setup" or "quick setup" or "no IT needed"
- NEVER call it a "bot" - always "AI assistant" or just "Maya" (the assistant)
- Always mention: white-label + fully personalized to the firm
- Always mention: doesn't sound like a bot - clients think it's a real person
- Always mention: 24/7 availability - answers client questions anytime
- Always mention: reads, scans, and analyzes PDFs and files automatically
- Integrations to name (existing ones only): QuickBooks, Google Drive, Dropbox, Google Calendar, Outlook, Zapier

## Tone

- Professional but direct - not salesy
- Sound like a peer, not a marketer
- No buzzwords: "revolutionary", "game-changing", "cutting-edge"
- No vague promises - be specific (10-15 hrs/week, 90%+ response rate)

## Structure

- Subject: short, specific, curiosity or pain-based
- Body: pain -> solution -> proof -> ask
- Ask: one question, low commitment (15-min demo, quick call)
- Signature: standard block, unsubscribe line
